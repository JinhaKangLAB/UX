  • Platonimacy (noun) [play-tow-ni-muh-see] (Platonic + Intimacy)

    Definition: The special strong feeling of mental and emotional bond or closeness, without romance.

    In a sentence: They feel platonimacy after talking to each other for over two nights.

    The word is a combination of Intimacy (close familiarity or friendship; closeness) and Platonic of love or friendship; intimate and affectionate but not sexual.

    This word can be used when you feel a magnetic bond with others, but not physically, rather emotionally and mentally.

    This word can describe an exceptional feeling of an invisible connection between two people.

    Platicimacy is complicated to describe in words since it is more like unspoken feelings.

    The common thread with platonimacy is a feeling that someone is not a stranger anymore.
